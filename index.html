Training material

This embeds the real YouTube video (via YouTube's own player) so you hear the actual speaker's voice — not synthetic speech. Captions are fetched automatically when available, to sync highlighting; if they're not available, copy the transcript manually ("..." → "Show transcript" under the video) and paste it into the "Your own text" tab, then still use the "YouTube video" voice engine below for real audio.

Your own recording or any audio/video file works. Playing back a real human voice gives students natural prosody that synthetic voices can't fully match.

Auto-transcription uses the same free Groq API key as the AI feedback feature below (get one at console.groq.com). With a key, word-level timing is captured too, enabling karaoke highlighting synced to your real audio.

02

Listening (Shadowing)

Browser voices vary a lot in quality. For the best free built-in voices, open this app in Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge. For the most natural prosody of all, use your own uploaded recording or a loaded YouTube video for real human speech.

Using your uploaded file for playback. Go to the "Upload audio / video" tab above to change it.

Playing the actual YouTube video (headphones recommended if you'll also record yourself, so the microphone doesn't pick up the video's own audio). Load a different video on the "YouTube / TED" tab above.

No captions for this video? Capture this tab's own audio and auto-transcribe it with Whisper (needs a free Groq key from Step 3, and Chrome or Edge). When the share dialog appears, choose "This Tab" and check "Share tab audio".

Get a free key at elevenlabs.io (free tier includes a monthly character quota). Stored only in your browser, used only for direct requests to ElevenLabs.

04

Interpretation practice (experimental)

Instead of repeating the same language, listen to the text loaded in Step 1 and speak your translation into the microphone — like a consecutive or simultaneous interpreter. Use headphones — otherwise the microphone may pick up the speaker audio itself and confuse recognition.

How it works

  1. Load a text (your own, from YouTube, or a demo).
  2. Listen at a speed suited to your level.
  3. Click a word to hear it and see a translation.
  4. Record yourself shadowing the speaker.
  5. Get a score and track your progress over time.
